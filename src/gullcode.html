Sample Questions

Tortise and the Hare

In a race to see who is the fastest, the tortoise and the hare face off. At the end of the race the tortoise crosses the finish line first. Because the hare was overconfident in his ability to win, he decided to run circles around the tortoise.

Task: Your job is to determine how many times (if any) the hare de- cided to run circles around the tortoise and determine the length of the cycle.

Specifications:

  1. The route of the hare will be given as a singly-linked list, the only part of the link list you will see is the address of the head.
  2. If there is a cycle you can assume that the hare was running a circle around the tortoise.
  3. Output should be integers separated by a space. The first number is the number of cycles, then following numbers are the length of each cycle (each one separated by a space).

Comma Sprinkler

As practice will tell you, the English rules for comma placement are complex, frustrating, and often ambiguous. Many people, especially mathematicians and computer scientists, will, in practice, ignore them, and, apply custom rules, or, no rules, at all. Dr. Comma Sprinkler solved this issue by developing a set of rules that sprinkles commas in a sentence with no ambiguity and little simplicity.

Task: Create an algorithm that applies her rules.

  1. If a word anywhere in the text is preceded by a comma, find all occurrences of that word in the text, and put a comma before each of those occurrences, except in the case where such an occurrence is the first word of a sentence or already preceded by a comma.
  2. If a word anywhere in the text is succeeded by a comma, find all occurrences of that word in the text, and put a comma after each of those occurrences, except in the case where such an occurrence is the last word of a sentence or already succeeded by a comma.
  3. Apply rules 1 and 2 repeatedly until no new commas can be added using either of them.

Specifications:

  1. Input will be one line of text with at least 2 and at most 1,000,000 characters
  2. Each character is either a lowercase letter, a comma, a period, or a space
  3. Each line of text will begin with a word, and between every two words there will either be a single space, a comma followed by a space, or a period followed by a space (denoting the end of a sentence and the beginning of a new one).

Specifications:

  1. The algorithm should minimize the worst-case behavior (Note: if you simply walk straight until you leave the forest, and the forest is very long, you may have a bad worst case scenario)
  2. Input will be in the form of a forest.h file that defines a black-box function we made
  3. Your code should be a test.cpp that invokes the isLost function to determine if Bellman is lost.
